## GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS

1. As per Rule 70(3) of the Copyright Rules, 2013, the application must be signed by the applicant (author/owner) only. An attorney is not permitted to sign **Form XIV**, the **Statement of Particulars (SoP)**, or the **Statement of Further Particulars (SoFP)** on behalf of the applicant.

2. **Form XIV**, **SoP**, and **SoFP** must be submitted only in the prescribed format. Only one copy of each is required.

3. If a photograph of any person appears on the work, provide the name, address, and nationality of such person in the remarks column. If such person is not the applicant, an original **No Objection Certificate (NOC)** must be enclosed (from heirs if deceased / guardian if minor).

4. The work must be identical to the **Search Certificate** with respect to size, colour, and design.

5. As per Rule 70(5), for registration of **Computer Programme/Software**, the applicant must provide **source code and object code**.

6. For **Sound Recording** and **Cinematograph Film** categories, enclose a copy of the agreement. If no agreement exists, obtain and enclose NOCs from all relevant copyright holders.

7. **Power of Attorney**, if any, must be on stamp paper, duly accepted by the attorney and signed by the applicant. Incomplete POA is not acceptable.

### STATEMENT OF PARTICULARS – GUIDANCE

- **Applicant** may be an individual, firm, company, government, university, or institution.
- **Nature of Interest** may be Author / Owner / Publisher / Composer / Producer / Artist.
- **Class of Work:** Literary / Artistic / Cinematograph Film / Sound Recording / Music / Computer Software.
- **Author** means the person(s) who actually created the work. Firms cannot be authors except for Cinematograph Films and Sound Recordings.
- If the author is not the applicant, submit an original **NOC** from the author(s).
- If the author is deceased, submit date of decease and a notarized affidavit regarding inheritance/NOC from legal heirs.
- For artistic works used or capable of being used in relation to goods, a **Search Certificate from the Trade Marks Registry** is mandatory (Rule 70(6)).
- If the work is neither used nor capable of being used in relation to goods, clearly state so.
