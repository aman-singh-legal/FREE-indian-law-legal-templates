## Case Law References

- Where in a divorce petition the wife took up a plea and also led evidence that the petitioner husband was interested in getting married with another girl, but the fact was not proved, it would not mean that the allegation was false which could amount to cruelty.  
  **Hema Reddy v. Rakesh Reddy**, 2002 (2) CCC 227 (A.P.)

- Once the ingredients of Section 13B of the Hindu Marriage Act are satisfied, the court has no other option except to accept the prayer for dissolution of marriage by decree of divorce.  
  **K. S. Subramanian v. V. Vasanthi Devi**, 2002 (2) CCC 153 (Mad.)

- Cruelty cannot be judged from a solitary incident but on an overall consideration of all relevant circumstances.  
  **G. V. N. Kameswara Rao v. G. Jabilli**, AIR 2002 SC 576

- The party seeking divorce on the ground of desertion is required to show that he or she was not taking advantage of his or her own wrong.  
  **Savitri Pandey v. Prem Chandra Pandey**, AIR 2002 SC 591
